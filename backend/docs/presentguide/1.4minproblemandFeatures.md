**"Every day, a loan officer at a bank in Nepal sits down and manually checks
50 to 200 KYC documents. One by one. Every field. By hand."**

*Pause one beat.*

- A single document review takes **5 to 15 minutes** — cross-checking names,
  citizenship numbers, PAN numbers, expiry dates, addresses across multiple papers.
- **1 in 10 documents** submitted to financial institutions contains an error,
  inconsistency, or outright fraud attempt.
- For a mid-size cooperative processing 100 documents a day, that is
  **10 fraudulent or incorrect documents slipping through — or being caught too late.**
- For manpower agencies: a worker's salary is promised on one document, a
  different amount appears on another. Destination country changes between forms.
  The agency doesn't catch it. The worker boards a flight to the wrong country
  for the wrong salary.
- The cost: **3–5 hours of skilled human labor per day**, per institution —
  labor that could be spent on customer service, not paperwork.

*This is not a technology problem. It is a process problem that technology has
not yet solved — in Nepal.*

## [0:45 – 1:30] THE SOLUTION — VIVAD X

*Switch to live dashboard or slide showing the verdict screen.*

---

**"VIVAD X verifies a KYC document in under 10 seconds."**

Here is exactly how it works:

- A company uploads 1 to 5 document images — citizenship, salary slip, letter,
  whatever the criteria requires.
- Our system extracts **structured fields** from every image in parallel using
  **Groq Llama 4 Scout vision AI** — one of the fastest and most accurate
  document-reading models available today.
- It then runs a **dynamic rule engine** across all extracted fields:
  - **Bank KYC** checks: Name, Father Name, Citizenship No., PAN No.,
    Expiry Date, Address, Amount — with configurable red/orange severity per rule.
  - **Manpower Agency** checks: Name, Agency Name, License No., Salary,
    Destination, Expiry Date, Payment Date, Deployment Date — including
    **date logic checks** (payment cannot be after deployment, that is impossible).
- The result is a **verdict in 3 colors**:
  - **Green** — all checks passed, document is clean.
  - **Orange** — one or more fields need human review.
  - **Red** — critical failure, document cannot be accepted.

*Each verdict comes with a risk score, a list of exactly which fields failed,
and why.*

## [2:15 – 2:45] TRUST & SECURITY LAYER

*This is the differentiator. Speak slowly here.*

---

**"Anyone can run AI on a document. Very few systems make that verification
permanent and tamper-proof."**

- Every document that receives a **Green verdict** is SHA-256 hashed — not
  just the image, but the **extracted field values** themselves — and that hash
  is written as a Tron blockchain transaction.
- The `txid` is stored in our database and returned in the API response.
- **Anyone** — the borrower, a regulator, an auditor — can go to
  `https://nile.tronscan.org/#/transaction/<txid>` and confirm the verification
  happened, when it happened, and what the document hash was.
- **No one can alter that record.** Not us. Not the bank. Not the borrower.

Additional trust layers:
- **API key authentication** for B2B integrations — no session tokens to steal,
  no cookie management, just a revocable server-side key.
- **Credit system** prevents abuse — every verification costs 1 credit.
  Zero-balance accounts are blocked before the AI call is made.

## Speaker Notes — Common Judge Questions

**"Why blockchain? Isn't a database enough?"**
A database can be altered by anyone with DB access — including us. The
blockchain record is controlled by no one. It is a public, timestamped,
cryptographic proof that a specific document with specific field values was
verified at a specific time. That is what a regulator or auditor needs.

**"What if the AI extracts a field wrong?"**
Orange verdict forces human review for exactly this reason. The system never
silently accepts a document when it is uncertain — it escalates. The human
makes the final call on Orange; the AI handles the Green and Red cases
automatically.

**"Why eSewa and not Khalti or cards?"**
eSewa has 7M+ registered users in Nepal — the largest user base. We will add
Khalti and Connect IPS in the next sprint. The payment framework is payment-
method-agnostic by design (`payment_method` table is dynamic).

**"Can this be used without the dashboard?"**
Yes. The entire platform is API-first. Banks can integrate `POST /api/v1/verify`
directly into their own loan management software using an API key. No dashboard
login needed.

**"What data do you store?"**
Document images are stored temporarily on the server. We store: extracted
field values (structured JSON), the SHA-256 hash, and the Tron txid. We do
not permanently store the raw images after verification. The company controls
their data and can delete document records.
