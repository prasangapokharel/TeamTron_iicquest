## SLIDE 2 — The Problem: Manual Document Verification  
**~90 seconds**

### On screen
**Do you know what these organizations still face every day?**

| Sector | What they verify manually |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **Banks & microfinance** | KYC forms, citizenship, PAN, salary slips, loan files |
| **Manpower agencies** | Worker contracts, licenses, destination & salary papers |
| **Consultancies** | Client IDs, contracts, compliance packs |
| **Universities** | Certificates, transcripts, admission documents |
| **Government** | Permits, subsidies, beneficiary records |

**The manual process hurts everywhere:**
1. **Slow** — 5–15 minutes per file; onboarding can take days  
2. **Expensive** — roughly **$3–8 per document** in labor (global benchmark)  
3. **Inconsistent** — different staff, different results; **4–15% error rates** on manual review  
4. **Fraud slips through** — fake bills, mismatched names, expired IDs still get approved  

**Global scale (2025–2026)**
- **~$7.8 billion** spent on KYC / identity verification in 2026 — growing **~16% per year**  
- **~$2.9 billion** spent on AML/KYC data & services in 2025 — up **12.3%** YoY; **22% CAGR** over the last 5 years  
- Document fraud in banking alone: **$20B+** global exposure; US commercial document fraud **~$12.5B** (2024)  
- Financial institution fraud losses projected **$23B (2025) → $58.3B (2030)** — **+153%**

**Nepal — this is not abstract**
- **106 licensed BFIs** (20 commercial banks, 17 development banks, 17 finance companies, 51 microfinance, 1 infrastructure bank) — NRB, mid-Jan 2026  
- **11,530+ branches** — every branch still touches paper KYC  
- **5.8 million** loan accounts queried for credit information in FY 2023/24 — each tied to identity documents  
- **NRB (Jan 2025):** National ID mandatory for all new accounts — more verification load, same manual teams  
- **NRB (2025):** Stricter AML/STR rules — banks must upgrade compliance; **AI-aided identity fraud** explicitly flagged  
- **Real alert (2024–25):** Fake “update your KYC” scams — proof that weak verification chains hurt real customers

> Banks, microfinance, consultancies, universities, and government offices all run on **documents**.

In one year, credit systems alone touched **5.8 million loan accounts** — all backed by paper identity. Since **January 2025**, National ID verification is **mandatory** for new accounts. Regulators are tightening AML rules — but the **process is still manual**.

## SLIDE 3 — Our Solution: VivadX  
**~90 seconds**

### On screen
**VivadX verifies documents in under 10 seconds.**

```
Upload → AI extracts fields → Rules engine → Verdict → Blockchain proof (if Green)
```

**What VivadX does**
1. **Upload** 1–5 images (KYC, certificates, contracts — any criteria you define)  
2. **Extract** every field with vision AI (Groq Llama 4 Scout) — parallel, structured JSON  
3. **Validate** with a dynamic rule engine — cross-match, expiry, thresholds, date logic  
4. **Verdict** — **Green** (pass) · **Orange** (human review) · **Red** (reject) + risk score 0–100  
5. **Prove** — Green documents hashed and signed on **Tron** — public, tamper-proof TXID  

**Built for Nepal’s real document types**
- Bank KYC · Manpower agency · University certificates · Custom criteria per company  

**Not a chatbot gimmick — a working system**
- REST API + dashboard · Credit-based SaaS · eSewa top-up · MCP for AI-native integrations  

### Say this
> "Our solution is **VivadX**.  
> A company selects its criteria — Bank KYC, Manpower, University — uploads documents, and in **under ten seconds** gets a structured result: every field extracted, every rule checked, every failure explained.  
>  
> **Green** means clean — we hash the extracted fields and anchor them on the **Tron blockchain**. Anyone can verify that proof on TronScan without logging into our system.  
> **Orange** means a human should review — we never silently accept uncertainty.  
> **Red** means reject — name mismatch, expired ID, citizenship conflict — flagged clearly, not buried in a PDF.  
>  
> This is **criteria-driven**, not hardcoded. Add a new document type by defining fields and rules — no code deploy.  
> Banks integrate via **API key**. Agencies use the **dashboard**. Regulators get **proof**, not promises."

## Judge Q&A — Quick Answers (backup, not slides)

**"Where did the billion-dollar numbers come from?"**  
Mordor Intelligence (KYC market), Burton-Taylor (AML/KYC spend), Juniper Research (fraud trajectory), NRB Annual Report (Nepal BFIs). Links are in Slide 2 sources table.

**"Why blockchain?"**  
A database can be edited. A Tron transaction is public and timestamped — auditors and regulators can verify **that specific fields were verified at a specific time** without trusting our word alone.

**"What if AI extracts wrong?"**  
Orange verdict escalates to human review. Red blocks acceptance. We automate Green and Red; humans handle Orange.

**"Nepal-specific?"**  
NRB mandates NID-linked KYC, 106+ BFIs, Devanagari/English mixed documents — our criteria engine and prompts are built for these forms.

**"Demo?"**  
"We can run a live verification after questions — upload KYC, show verdict and Tron signature in under ten seconds."
